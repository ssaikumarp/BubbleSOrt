WHITE BLOOD CELLS

White blood cells are one of the components of our blood, along with red blood cells, platelets and plasma. Each of these components has a unique function in our body and in medicine. White blood cells are continuously made in our bone marrow. They have a short lifespan of just one to three days. The medical term for these cells is leukocytes. There are several different types of cells, including granulocytes, monocytes and lymphocytes.

What Do White Blood Cells Do?

White blood cells help our bodies defend against disease in three ways:

1. By destroying harmful bacteria.

2. By creating antibodies against bacteria and viruses.

3. By fighting malignant disease.
